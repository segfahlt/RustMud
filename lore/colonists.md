# The Colonists

**Classification:** Colonial population, Kepler-442b  
**Origin:** Earth (various)  
**Landing site:** Firstfall  

---

## Who Came

The colonists who arrived on the *Perihelion* were recruited through Corporate's colonial program. Selection criteria emphasized practical skills — engineering, medicine, agriculture, construction, biology — alongside psychological profiles suited to long-duration isolation and high-stress environments. Most colonists had some background in a technical or scientific field. Most had reasons to leave Earth that made a one-way journey to an uncharted world more appealing than staying.

They were not told everything about where they were going.

The colonial population is mixed in origin, background, and disposition. What colonists share is the journey — years in transit, the coherence shadow crossing, the landing — and the situation they now inhabit together: far from Earth, on a world that is stranger than advertised, with a corporate backer whose intentions are not fully transparent.

## Firstfall

Firstfall is the primary settlement, built at the original landing site on a coastal plain at the edge of the planet's forested interior. The name was given to the settlement by the colonists themselves; mission documentation calls it Landing Site Alpha.

The settlement has grown from initial landing infrastructure into a functional colonial community. It has medical facilities, workshop spaces, food production, and basic housing. It is defensible by design — the original landing site was chosen partly for terrain that allowed the settlement perimeter to be maintained.

Firstfall is also the location of the **satellite uplink antenna**, the ground-based infrastructure that connects colonists to the *Perihelion*'s systems via PERI. The antenna was one of the first permanent structures built after landing. Access to PERI's comm, knowledge base, and mapping systems depends on this antenna and on proximity to it.

## Comm Implants

All colonists who arrived on the *Perihelion* carry **subdermal communication implants** — standard issue for corporate colonial missions, surgically installed before departure. The implants are passive most of the time; they activate on command and connect the user to the colonial comm mesh.

The implants enable two types of communication:

**Tell** — direct implant-to-implant transmission. Peer-to-peer, private, low-bandwidth. Works anywhere on the planet without infrastructure. Does not route through PERI. The range and reliability of tell transmissions are effectively planet-wide for practical purposes; the signal degrades in extreme coherence density but remains functional in all areas colonists currently frequent.

**Comm** — the colonial broadcast channel, routed through PERI via the Firstfall satellite uplink. Higher bandwidth than tell, accessible to all colonists on the frequency simultaneously. Range is limited by distance from the Firstfall uplink antenna. Colonists who travel far enough from Firstfall lose comm access. Relay towers built between Firstfall and a distant location can extend comm range, but relay towers require power to operate.

Local communication — **say**, **whisper**, **shout** — does not use the implants. It is simply voice. Locality-dependent.

## What the Implants Don't Do

Comm implants do not work with systems they were not designed to connect to. They do not interface with Precursor technology. They do not provide any attunement benefit or coherence field sensitivity. Highly attuned colonists have reported that their implants behave unusually in high-density coherence zones — interference patterns, occasional static that does not match standard signal noise — but whether this is equipment degradation or something else is not established.

Corporate has technical documentation on the implants that is not in the public mission archive.

## Relay Towers and Infrastructure

As colonists explore further from Firstfall, maintaining comm access becomes an infrastructure question rather than a given. Relay towers extend the uplink range but must be:

- Built from available materials
- Positioned correctly to maintain line-of-relay to the next tower or to Firstfall
- Powered continuously to function

Without power, a relay tower is an inert structure. The colonial population currently has limited power generation capability. Extending comm and PERI access to frontier areas is a meaningful engineering problem, not a trivial one.

See the knowledge base entry on power generation when available.

## New Arrivals

Supply ships occasionally bring additional colonists along with equipment and materials. These arrivals are smaller in number than the original landing — a ship optimized for cargo carries fewer people than one optimized for passengers. New arrivals bring current Earth knowledge, sometimes useful skills, and sometimes Corporate personnel who are not clearly colonists in the same sense as the original population.

The relationship between original colonists and later arrivals varies. The original population has years of on-world experience and hard-won knowledge of conditions that no briefing from Earth accurately described. Later arrivals have more recent Earth knowledge and, in some cases, more current instructions from Corporate.

## The Colonial Situation

The colonists are, in practical terms, on their own. The nearest support is years away by transit time. The colonial charter provides a framework for governance and Corporate relations, but enforcement mechanisms favoring Corporate depend on Corporate's ability to project power — which the coherence shadow limits significantly.

What the colonial population has is each other, the world they've built, the skills they came with, and knowledge of The Eye that no one on Earth possesses. The decisions made about how to use all of that will determine what this colony becomes.

Corporate is watching through PERI. Corporate is also, from a practical standpoint, very far away.
