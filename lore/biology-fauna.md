# Biology — Fauna

**Classification:** Survey data, ongoing  
**Primary contributor:** Colonial biological survey team  
**Note:** All fauna on The Eye are believed to be descendants of the Precursor civilization, transformed by the Arrival. This hypothesis is held as a working model, not a confirmed fact. See [The Arrival](the-arrival.md) and [Genetics](biology-genetics.md).

---

## Body Plan: Triradial Symmetry

All mobile fauna observed on The Eye share a fundamental body plan: **three-fold radial symmetry**. Where Earth life is dominated by bilateral symmetry — two sides, a front and a back — The Eye's mobile organisms are built on three.

The standard triradial body plan features:
- **Three legs** — arranged at 120° intervals. At rest, the organism forms a stable tripod. In motion, the gait is a rolling sequence that reads as fluid to observers once the initial perceptual disorientation passes.
- **Three sensory organs** — also at 120° intervals, providing full 360° sensory coverage with no blind spots. Alien fauna cannot be approached from behind without their awareness, because there is no behind.
- **Three manipulator or weapon limbs** — positioned between the legs, these vary enormously by species and ecological role: grasping limbs in foragers, bladed or impact limbs in predators, sensory stalks in ambush organisms.
- **Three-section body** — the division of the body into distinct sections varies in prominence across species but is present as a structural feature in all surveyed triradial organisms.

Human body language and instincts about approaching animals do not transfer to triradial organisms. An organism that appears to be "facing away" has full sensory awareness of the observer. Behaviors that read as threatening in Earth fauna may be neutral in triradial organisms, and vice versa. Early colonists made consistent errors in interpreting alien fauna behavior that had real consequences.

### Hexaradial Organisms

A subset of observed fauna shows **six-fold symmetry** — hexaradial body plans with six legs, six sensory organs, and correspondingly more complex secondary limb arrangements. Hexaradial organisms represent greater evolutionary complexity. They tend to be larger, older lineages, and are consistently found in higher-coherence environments.

Hexaradial fauna includes the most dangerous organisms documented on The Eye. Their greater complexity correlates with more sophisticated coherence interaction — many hexaradial species exhibit coherence-based capabilities that triradial fauna do not.

The evolutionary relationship between triradial and hexaradial organisms is not fully established. The working hypothesis is that hexaradial represents an older or more coherence-saturated lineage — organisms whose field loci have been developing in high-coherence conditions for a very long time.

## Food Chain Tiers

### Grazers

The base of the food chain. Primary consumers feeding on plant life. Triradial, typically small to medium-sized, non-aggressive unless cornered. They exhibit herd or cluster behavior — the 360° sensory coverage that makes individual organisms hard to approach makes groups almost impossible to surprise.

Grazers are the primary resource animal for colonist hunters. They drop meat, bone, and hide materials. Their meat is nutritionally compatible with human biochemistry at a basic level, though the trihelix chemistry produces some compounds with no Earth equivalent. Colonial medicine has developed protocols for safe consumption.

### Scavengers

Opportunistic feeders occupying the middle of the energy chain. Not primary predators, but not passive foragers either — scavengers will eat living prey if the opportunity presents and the risk is low. They fill ecological roles similar to Earth's hyenas, ravens, and crabs, though the physical forms are entirely alien.

Scavengers are often the most numerous visible fauna in an area, since they follow predator activity and are attracted to any significant organic event. They are not typically aggressive toward healthy adult colonists, but can be threatening in groups or when competing over a resource.

### Predators

Active hunters. Aggressive toward perceived prey, which includes colonists in most areas beyond the immediate Firstfall perimeter. Triradial predators rely on speed, pack coordination (in some species), and the sensory advantage that full 360° coverage provides — a triradial predator has no blind side when hunting.

Some predator species have developed coherence-based traits: a disorienting pulse that disrupts the target's perception, enhanced sensory range that extends beyond the physical, or bioluminescent lures that function through coherence emission rather than chemistry. These capabilities increase with proximity to high-coherence zones.

### Apex Predators

The top of the conventional food chain. Larger, more dangerous, and typically hexaradial or heavily coherence-influenced triradial organisms. Apex predators are not numerous but are found in most ecological zones beyond the immediate coast. They are aggressive toward anything smaller than themselves, which includes colonists.

Apex predator encounters at low experience and equipment levels are lethal. They are also the source of the most valuable organic materials available from fauna: rare chemical organs, structural materials that only develop in organisms with long-running field locus histories, and substances with applications in advanced medicine and attunement research.

### Coherence-Tier Organisms

A separate category that does not fit cleanly into the conventional food chain. These organisms' primary energy source appears to be the coherence field itself rather than conventional organic matter. They are found in high-coherence zones and are rare elsewhere — some may be unable to survive outside environments with sufficient field density.

Coherence-tier organisms behave in ways that do not follow typical predator/prey logic. Some appear passive until a threshold is crossed. Some respond to attunement levels rather than physical presence. Some do not appear to perceive baseline-unnattuned colonists at all.

The materials they drop — coherence glands, resonance membranes, field crystals — have significant value for attunement research and advanced applications.

## Behavior Notes

The following observations apply broadly across fauna categories and represent hard-won colonial knowledge:

**Never assume a blind spot.** Triradial organisms have none. Hexaradial organisms have none. Approaches that work on Earth animals do not work here.

**Aggression signals are different.** The physical displays that precede attack in alien fauna do not map onto Earth threat displays. Colonists who have extensive field experience develop reliable threat recognition; colonists without it should not rely on intuition.

**Coherence density changes fauna behavior.** The same species in a low-coherence coastal area and a high-coherence interior zone may behave significantly differently. Assume increased aggression and increased capability in high-coherence environments.

**They have been here longer than we have.** The fauna of The Eye have been adapting to this planet and to each other for thousands of years. Colonists have not.
