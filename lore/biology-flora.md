# Biology — Flora

**Classification:** Survey data, ongoing  
**Primary contributor:** Colonial biological survey team, colonial medicine  
**Note:** Native plants are sessile organisms sharing the same trihelix/field locus genetic architecture as fauna. They are not a separate evolutionary tree — they diverged from the same ancestral organisms early in The Eye's biological history.

---

## First Impressions

The vegetation of The Eye is immediately, visually alien. There is no green. The primary photosynthetic pigments of native plants absorb the orange-red output of Kepler-442 efficiently; they reflect very little of that spectrum back. The result is a palette of deep purples, red-blacks, and dark burgundies, with some species ranging into near-black.

Colonists consistently describe the interior vegetation as visually oppressive in the early adjustment period — not because it is hostile in the moment, but because every trained instinct about what plants look like is wrong. The eye keeps looking for green and keeps not finding it. Adaptation takes time.

The second impression, after the visual, is the coherence emission. Colonists with developing attunement perceive it before they can identify what they're perceiving: a low-level background presence that is denser in thick plant growth than in clearings, and densest of all in ancient growth zones near ruin sites. The plants are not merely passive organisms. They are active participants in the coherence field.

## Body Plan: Pentaradial and Spiral

Native plants and sessile organisms show **five-fold symmetry** (pentaradial) or **spiral growth patterns**. This distinguishes them visually from mobile fauna — a triradial organism and a pentaradial plant look entirely different at a glance, which reduces misidentification errors in the field.

Pentaradial ground cover, spike-growths, and mid-height structural plants are the most common forms. Spiral-growth species tend toward larger, more complex forms and are more common in high-coherence zones where their resonance cycling has more field energy to draw on.

## Dual Metabolism

Native plants use two distinct energy systems simultaneously.

### Sunlight Metabolism

Standard photosynthesis, optimized for the K-type star's orange-red output spectrum. Input: light. Output: sugars, oils, structural compounds, and the standard organic chemistry that underlies biological material. This is the energy system Earth science understands, applied to an alien context.

The products of sunlight metabolism are broadly compatible with Earth biochemistry at the molecular level, with important caveats. Alien plant matter contains novel compounds — products of the trihelix amino acid set — with no Earth equivalent. Colonial medicine has characterized most common species for safety; unknown species should be treated with caution.

### Resonance Cycling

The second energy system has no Earth analogue. Native plants draw energy from the coherence field through the **field locus** component of their genetics — the coherence-stored half of the alien genome. This process functions analogously to how Earth plants use CO₂: input from the environment, chemical transformation, byproduct release.

The byproduct of resonance cycling is **coherence emission**. Plants breathe the field and exhale it, amplified.

This creates a feedback loop: dense plant growth increases local coherence density, which enables more productive resonance cycling, which supports denser growth. High-coherence zones are heavily vegetated by organisms feeding on the available field energy. Clearing vegetation in a zone causes coherence to gradually decline over time. Precursor ruins, which emit residual field energy, are overgrown with the most potent plant life on the planet — they have been feeding the growth above them for thousands of years.

### What Bioluminescence Actually Is

The bioluminescence observed in native plant life — the faint glow visible in dense vegetation at night, the pulsing in ancient growth zones — is coherence emission, not chemical light production. It is not in the visible spectrum for unnattuned observers, who perceive it as an indirect glow or a quality of the darkness that is hard to describe. Colonists with developing attunement perceive it directly, with increasing clarity as attunement develops. Field-sensitive instruments can also detect it.

The bioluminescence of individual organisms is a coherence signature as well as light. Some predator species have evolved to read these signatures as information.

## Plant Resources

Native plants are a significant resource base for the colony. Colonial medicine and chemistry have been working since landing to characterize what the biosphere provides.

### Sunlight Metabolites

The organic products of conventional plant metabolism. These are in most respects familiar chemistry in unfamiliar forms:

- **Food base** — many native plants produce edible material compatible with human digestion at a basic level. Flavor profiles are entirely alien; some colonists adapt to them readily, some do not. Safety varies by species and preparation.
- **Structural fiber** — native stalks and integument-analogues provide fibrous material usable as rope, fabric, and reinforcement. Properties vary; some are exceptionally strong relative to weight.
- **Fuel** — alien plant oils and dried organic matter burn cleanly and have been used as fuel where equipment permits.
- **Alcohol base** — fermentable sugars exist in several common species. Colonial brewing started within the first season. This fact is documented without editorial comment.
- **Basic organics** — raw material for chemistry, medicine, and fabrication. The trihelix amino acid set means that alien organic chemistry has a different toolkit than Earth chemistry, but the toolkit is large.

### Resonance Metabolites

The products of resonance cycling are different in kind from sunlight metabolites. They are partially or entirely coherence-field-mediated compounds — substances that exist because a field locus has been processing alien energy. Some are only chemically stable in the presence of the coherence field; remove them from a high-density zone and they begin to break down.

These materials represent the frontier of colonial medicine and chemistry:

- **Medicines** — certain resonance metabolites interact with human biology in ways that produce significant medical effects. The mechanisms are not understood from first principles; colonial medicine has developed empirical protocols based on observed effects. Some are beneficial in ways that no Earth compound matches. Some are dangerous.
- **Psychoactive compounds** — some resonance metabolites interact specifically with the developing attunement responses in human neurology. Effects range from temporarily enhanced coherence perception to extended altered states to documented psychological crises. These compounds are not banned by colonial authority but their use is considered a serious decision.
- **Poisons** — some resonance metabolites are toxic to organisms with field loci (native fauna) but harmless to baseline-unnattuned humans. Highly attuned colonists may also be vulnerable as their own field locus develops. The mechanism is direct field locus disruption.
- **Advanced materials** — some resonance metabolites have properties — conductivity, coherence interaction, structural characteristics — that make them useful in ways colonial engineering is still characterizing.

### Ruin-Zone Flora

Plants growing above Precursor ruin sites have been drawing on residual coherence emission from the ruins for thousands of years. They are, on average, the most potent sources of resonance metabolites available. They are also in some cases found nowhere else. Ruin-zone flora is a significant incentive for ruin exploration beyond the architectural and relic value.

Ruin-zone plants can also be dangerous in ways that standard interior flora is not. Extended exposure to Precursor coherence frequencies produces growth patterns and chemical profiles that have no analogue in the wider biosphere. Some ruin-zone plants respond to attunement levels in ways that suggest they are not entirely passive.

## Field Notes

Colonists should be aware that the plant life of The Eye is not background. It is active. It interacts with the coherence field continuously. It emits field energy. High-coherence plant-dense zones are environments where the boundary between the biological and the coherence field is thin in ways that have practical consequences for navigation, equipment, and attunement development.

Unknown species should not be consumed, handled without protection, or harvested without characterization. The biosphere of The Eye rewards careful observation and punishes assumption.
