# The Eye — Kepler-442b

**Classification:** Terrestrial exoplanet  
**Star:** Kepler-442 (K-type, orange dwarf)  
**Distance from Earth:** ~1,200 light-years  
**Mission designation:** Primary colonization target, *Perihelion* Mission  

---

## Overview

Kepler-442b — known to colonists as **The Eye** — is the primary setting of human habitation in this star system. The name emerged organically among the colonists shortly after landing. Its origin is disputed: some attribute it to the planet's unusual atmospheric optical properties, which create a faint ring effect around certain high-altitude storms. Others suggest it refers to the persistent sense that the planet is observing its new inhabitants.

PERI does not have an official position on the name's origin. Both explanations are plausible.

## Star and Conditions

Kepler-442 is a K-type orange dwarf, cooler and longer-lived than Earth's sun. Its output peaks in the orange-red spectrum rather than yellow-green. The practical consequences for the surface of The Eye are significant:

- **Light quality:** The sky reads as deeper amber during the day, shifting toward burnt orange at dusk. Shadows are softer. Human eyes adapt within weeks, though early colonists reported persistent color perception shifts.
- **Vegetation pigmentation:** Native plant life does not use chlorophyll. Primary photosynthetic pigments absorb orange-red wavelengths efficiently; the result is vegetation in deep purples, red-blacks, and dark burgundies. The landscape contains no green. Colonists consistently describe it as visually disorienting in ways that persist longer than expected.
- **UV levels:** Lower than Earth-standard due to stellar output profile. Human skin protection requirements are reduced, though coherence field exposure introduces different long-term health considerations (see [Coherence Field](coherence-field.md)).

## Gravity and Atmosphere

Gravity is approximately 0.84g — meaningfully lighter than Earth. Colonists adapt quickly and report it as a minor physical benefit. Long-term colonists show some reduction in bone density and muscle mass that stabilizes after the first year.

The atmosphere is breathable without assistance. Nitrogen-oxygen composition within survivable parameters. Colonists are advised that certain coherence-dense zones may produce atmospheric anomalies — localized pressure fluctuations, electromagnetic interference — that are not yet fully characterized.

## Geography

Surface geography has been partially mapped via orbital survey (see `wmap`). Ground-truth data from colonist exploration is continuously improving the map's resolution. The following are known at the time of this entry:

**Firstfall** is the landing site and primary settlement. Located on a coastal plain at the edge of a forested interior. Chosen for its relatively low coherence field density (by the standards of this planet) and access to fresh water and defensible terrain.

**The interior** rises into older, heavily vegetated terrain. Coherence field density increases with distance from the coast. Precursor ruins have been identified at multiple interior sites via orbital scan.

**The debris field** — a region of impact scatter from the original asteroid event, located some distance from Firstfall. Contains both raw materials and hazardous unstable terrain. Early colonists discovered that the debris carries residual coherence signatures inconsistent with simple geological impact material.

**Coastal regions** extending from Firstfall are the lowest-risk exploration zone for new colonists. Coherence density is lower, fauna is less aggressive, and the terrain is more predictable.

## Coherence Field Distribution

The planet's coherence field is not uniform. Density varies significantly by location and correlates with several factors: proximity to Precursor ruins, density of native vegetation, geological features that may correspond to ancient Precursor infrastructure, and the debris field's residual signatures.

Colonists moving inland from Firstfall will encounter progressively higher coherence density. High-coherence zones are both more dangerous and more resource-rich. Navigation in dense coherence zones degrades standard electronic equipment. Field-sensitive colonists (those with developing attunement) report additional perceptual effects.

See [The Coherence Field](coherence-field.md) for full documentation.

## A Note on the Name

PERI has observed that colonists who spend extended time on The Eye develop a strong emotional relationship with the planet that is not typical of colonial psychology in other mission profiles PERI has on record. Whether this is a function of isolation, the unusual aesthetic environment, or something the coherence field does to long-term residents, PERI cannot say with confidence.

The name "The Eye" may simply be accurate.
