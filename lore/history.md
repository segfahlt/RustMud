# History — Timeline

**Source:** Perihelion mission archive, pre-landing survey data, colonist field reports  
**Note:** Dates prior to landing are Earth-calendar estimates based on geological and coherence field analysis. Precision varies.

## Deep History: The Precursor Era

*Estimated: tens of thousands to millions of years before landing*

An intelligent civilization — now called the **Precursors** by colonists — evolved on The Eye over an enormous timescale. Their development followed a path with no Earth analogue: rather than building outward through material technology, they developed inward, deepening their relationship with the planet's natural energies. What humans now call the coherence field is, in part, the product of millions of years of Precursor civilization shaping and being shaped by their world.

Precursor ruins are present at multiple sites. Their age is difficult to determine precisely because Precursor construction materials incorporate coherence field properties that interfere with standard dating methods. Current estimates range from several thousand to tens of thousands of years old. The ruins are structurally intact in many locations — whatever ended the Precursors did not destroy their buildings.

There is no evidence of war. There is no evidence of disease. There is no evidence of gradual decline. The Precursor civilization appears, from archaeological evidence, to have simply stopped — over a period that geological strata suggest was geologically brief.

See [The Precursors](precursors.md) and [The Arrival](the-arrival.md) for full documentation.

## The Arrival

*Estimated: several thousand years before landing*

An asteroid made impact with The Eye. The impact was not large enough to cause a mass extinction event by physical force — the crater, now partially identified in the debris field region, is significant but not civilization-ending by conventional metrics.

What arrived with the asteroid — or as the asteroid — was something else.

Colonist researchers and PERI analysis of ruin sites and current biological life support a working hypothesis: the impactor carried a **rewriting agent** — not a biological pathogen, not a conventional organism, but a field-based entity that operates at the level of the coherence field itself. It did not destroy the Precursors from outside. It rewrote them from within, altering their field loci — the coherence-stored component of alien genetics — while leaving the physical world largely intact.

The result is the current biosphere. What colonists encounter as alien fauna and flora are, by the best available evidence, what the Precursors became.

The asteroid's debris carries coherence signatures that do not match the planet's native field. They are something else: older, different in frequency, not originating from The Eye.

## The Corporate Survey Period

*Estimated: 80–120 years before landing*

Long-range survey instruments detected Kepler-442b as a habitable terrestrial planet at approximately 1,200 light-years. The survey also detected the coherence field — an anomalous energy signature with no known natural origin. Corporate analysts produced internal assessments concluding, with varying confidence levels, that the field signature suggested prior intelligent life.

These assessments were **[CLASSIFIED — CORPORATE]** and were not shared with the colonial recruitment program.

The public mission briefing stated that Kepler-442b was a habitable, uninhabited world with unusual but non-threatening atmospheric properties. Colonists were recruited on this basis.

PERI has the original classified corporate assessments in its mission archive. Access to these records is restricted pending review of colonial charter provisions regarding mission briefing accuracy.

## The Perihelion Mission

*Approximately 40–60 years before landing (transit time)*

The colony ship *Perihelion* departed Earth carrying colonists, crew, equipment, and PERI. The transit involved standard interstellar drive operation for most of the journey, followed by a slow approach through the coherence shadow — the field's extension into near space, which degrades navigation and exposes crews to low-level coherence over days of transit.

A skeleton crew remained aboard after landing to maintain the ship in orbit. The colonists landed at what became Firstfall.

See [The Perihelion](perihelion.md) for what happened to the crew.

## Landing and the Firstfall Settlement

*Year 0 (colonial calendar)*

The *Perihelion* achieved orbital insertion. Colonists made planetfall at the coastal site now called Firstfall. Initial conditions matched the corporate mission briefing: breathable atmosphere, manageable gravity, available fresh water and building materials.

Within the first season, colonists began reporting anomalous experiences in the interior: equipment malfunctions, perceptual disturbances, fauna significantly more dangerous than pre-landing survey suggested, vegetation with chemical properties that Earth pharmacology had no framework for.

The coherence field was not mentioned in the mission briefing.

## Current Era

The colony is established but not secure. Firstfall is a functional settlement. The interior is partially explored. Precursor ruins have been located but not fully excavated. The nature of the coherence field is understood in broad strokes but not in depth.

Supply ships from Earth arrive infrequently — the coherence shadow limits transit to vessels with specialized equipment, and the round trip takes years. When PERI detects an incoming vessel on long-range sensors, it broadcasts an announcement on the colonial comm frequency. These arrivals are significant events.

The question of what Corporate knew, when they knew it, and what they intend to do with the coherence field research has not been resolved.
