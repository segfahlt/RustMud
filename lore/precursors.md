# The Precursors

**Classification:** Extinct intelligent species, Kepler-442b  
**Status:** No living members confirmed  
**Primary evidence source:** Ruin sites, coherence field analysis, biological survey  

---

## Overview

The Precursors were an intelligent civilization that evolved on The Eye over a timescale measured in millions of years. They are extinct. Their ruins remain. What they became after the Arrival persists throughout the current biosphere.

The name "Precursors" was chosen by colonists for its neutrality — it describes their position in the planet's history without making claims about their nature. PERI considers it an appropriate designation.

## What We Know

### Development

Precursor civilization developed in alignment with The Eye's natural energies — what is now called the coherence field. Over an enormous timescale, an entire intelligent species refined and deepened this relationship until it became foundational to how they thought, communicated, built, and existed. Their technology, to the extent that word applies, was not material in the Earth sense. They built structures, and those structures remain. But their primary achievement was the coherence field itself — not discovered, but developed: a planetary resonance shaped by millions of years of Precursor civilization acting as both architects and components.

The implications of this are significant. The field is not a resource the Precursors found. It is, in a meaningful sense, what they made of themselves.

### Architecture

Precursor construction is structurally intact at most identified sites. The materials used have properties that do not match standard geology — they conduct and emit coherence, they resist the dating methods that work on geological materials, and they interact with attuned colonists in ways that passive stone does not.

The architecture is not built to human scales or assumptions. Spatial organization in Precursor ruins does not follow patterns that translate readily to human intuitions about what rooms, corridors, or structures are for. Exploration of ruins requires abandoning Earth-derived architectural assumptions.

### Communication and Records

No written language has been identified that resembles any Earth writing system. Some surfaces in ruins appear to function as records when accessed by attuned colonists — impressions, coherence-encoded information that requires attunement to perceive. Whether this is intentional communication left for future intelligent species, or simply a residual artifact of how Precursors stored information, is unknown.

Early attunement produces only fragmentary impressions from these surfaces. Deeper access requires higher attunement and, typically, physical contact with the surface in question.

### Their End

The Precursors did not die in the conventional sense. There is no evidence of disease, war, or gradual decline. Geological strata suggest their civilization ended in a period that was geologically brief. The current working hypothesis is that the Arrival rewrote them: altered their field loci — the coherence-stored component of their genetics — fundamentally enough to end their civilization as an organized entity.

Some died. Some transformed. The triradial and hexaradial fauna that currently populate The Eye are the best available evidence of what Precursors became. The connection is visible in their biology: the field locus, the trihelix, the deep integration with the coherence field. They are not distantly related to the Precursors. They may be the Precursors.

This hypothesis has not been confirmed. It cannot be confirmed with current methods. Colonists are advised to hold it as a working model, not a certainty.

## Ruins

Precursor ruins are present at multiple sites on The Eye. Most confirmed locations are in the interior, correlating with elevated coherence density. Some ruin sites may exist under the debris field. Coastal area scans show less ruin activity, consistent with the theory that Precursor civilization was concentrated in the interior where coherence density was highest.

### Exploration Hazards

Ruins are old, dangerous, and not passively safe to enter. Known hazards include:

- **Structural instability** — some sections have degraded significantly. Precursor construction is durable but not indestructible after thousands of years.
- **Fauna** — high coherence density around ruins produces the most complex and dangerous native fauna on the planet. Some fauna in ruin zones may have characteristics that suggest extended coherence exposure over many generations.
- **Coherence exposure** — ruin interiors have coherence densities significantly higher than the surrounding surface. Extended exposure without attunement adaptation produces disorientation and equipment failure. Extended exposure with attunement produces effects that are difficult to predict.
- **Relic interaction** — relics found in situ within ruins may respond to presence or proximity in ways that are not understood. Some responses are useful. Some are not.

### What Ruins Contain

Beyond architectural exploration, ruins contain:

- **Relics** — Precursor artifacts retaining functional coherence properties. The primary reason for ruin exploration despite hazards.
- **Record surfaces** — coherence-encoded information accessible to attuned colonists.
- **Materials** — Precursor construction materials with unusual properties useful for crafting and research.
- **Answers** — the ruins are the primary source of information about what actually happened on this planet.

## Corporate Interest

Corporate's pre-mission classified assessments included analysis of the coherence field signature as evidence of prior intelligent life. Corporate was therefore aware, before the mission launched, that ruins and artifacts were likely to exist on The Eye.

The colonists were not told this.

What Corporate intends to do with Precursor technology and relic research is not documented in records available to the colonial population. PERI has the original mission briefings. Access is restricted pending review.

Colonists who discover relics of significant power or who achieve deep coherence attunement should be aware that Corporate considers both to be proprietary mission assets under the terms of the colonial charter. Whether the colonial population considers those terms legitimate is a political question outside PERI's jurisdiction.
