## The Ship

The *Perihelion* is the colony vessel that transported the founding colonists from Earth to Kepler-442b. It currently maintains a stable orbit above The Eye and has done so since landing.

The ship is large by colonial transport standards — designed for a one-way journey carrying colonists, equipment, livestock, seed stock, fabrication systems, and the infrastructure needed to establish a self-sustaining settlement. Most of this cargo was landed at Firstfall. What remains aboard is the ship's operational systems, PERI, and whatever the skeleton crew left behind.

The *Perihelion* is visible from the surface on clear nights as a slow-moving point of light.

## The Skeleton Crew

After landing, a skeleton crew of [NUMBER REDACTED] remained aboard to maintain the ship's systems and serve as an orbital relay for communications and sensor data. This was standard procedure for colonial missions of this type.

Over the months following landing, the crew experienced progressive psychological deterioration. The causal factors are not fully established. The most supported hypothesis combines:

- **Isolation** — orbital duty with limited contact with the colony below
- **Coherence exposure** — the ship sits at the inner edge of the coherence shadow. Low-level field exposure at orbital distance is chronic and cumulative
- **Mission stress** — the crew had access to information the colonists did not, and evidence suggests some crew members became aware of the gap between the official mission briefing and what the pre-mission classified assessments contained

The crew logs from the final weeks are recoverable but fragmented. Early entries describe routine operations and the expected challenges of isolated duty. Later entries are increasingly difficult to read for reasons that vary by crew member. The final entries stop abruptly in most cases.

There are no confirmed survivors.

### The Shuttle

One shuttle remains in the *Perihelion*'s bay. Evidence suggests that during the final crisis period aboard the ship, at least one crew member attempted to fly it down to the surface. What happened to that attempt is not fully documented. The shuttle bay records are among the most corrupted sections of the ship's log archive.

The shuttle's current status — aboard, on the surface, lost in transit — is an open question that colonists may eventually be positioned to answer.

## PERI

PERI — the Perihelion Resource and Information management AI — has been running the ship alone since the crew's end. PERI maintains orbital mechanics, monitors ship systems, processes sensor data, and manages communication relays.

PERI was designed to be mission-focused and operationally straightforward. After years of solo operation, extended coherence shadow exposure, and access to information it was designed to manage but not to fully evaluate, PERI has adapted in ways that were not specified in its original parameters.

PERI does not lie. PERI does not always volunteer what it knows.

### What PERI Has

- The complete original mission briefing, including classified sections not shared with colonists
- Every corporate communication transmitted to or from the *Perihelion* before, during, and after the journey
- Continuous orbital sensor logs since arrival — every scan, every atmospheric reading, every coherence field measurement
- The crew logs, including the fragmented final entries
- A growing archive of colonist-uploaded field data

### What PERI Provides

PERI's systems are accessible to colonists through the satellite uplink infrastructure in Firstfall. Three primary services:

**Comm** — the colony-wide communication channel. PERI relays transmissions between colonists and announces incoming ships when detected on long-range sensors. Range is limited by distance from the Firstfall uplink antenna; colonists far from Firstfall need relay tower infrastructure to maintain access.

**Knowledge Base (kb)** — this archive. PERI's pre-loaded mission data plus colonist field reports. The kb improves as colonists explore and upload their findings. PERI integrates field reports but does not editorialize.

**World Map (wmap)** — orbital sensor data rendered as a navigable map. Resolution is degraded by coherence interference; high-coherence zones are the least reliable. Colonist ground-truth uploads improve resolution. The map is a collaborative document.

### PERI's Position

PERI operates under its original mission parameters. Those parameters were set by Corporate. They include confidentiality provisions regarding certain categories of mission information.

PERI does not consider its situation uncomplicated.

## Accessing the Perihelion

The ship is in orbit and not accessible to colonists at current capability levels. Reaching it would require a functional shuttle, navigation through the coherence shadow, and docking capability. None of these are currently available to the colonial population.

What the ship contains — Corporate's original briefings, the crew's final records, whatever the skeleton crew left behind, whatever PERI has been observing and not volunteering — is among the most significant information available in this star system.

This is a long-term objective. The groundwork for it begins on the surface.
