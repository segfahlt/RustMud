# Firstfall (Port Meridian)

Status: **Draft** — facilities defined, room layout and descriptions pending

---

## Overview

Firstfall is the landing base and the only permanent human settlement of any size on the planet. Corporate calls it Port Meridian. Everyone else calls it Firstfall — named for what happened to the first wave of colonists who touched this ground.

It is simultaneously a Corporate administrative hub, a frontier town, a black market, and the only place on the planet where you can get a real meal, a real doctor, and a locked door between you and what's outside the walls.

**Age:** 1 year old. The wall is up. The prefabs are standing. It does not yet feel permanent.
**Population:** ~700 permanent residents. The first year was brutal — not everyone who landed is still here.
**Physical structure:** Corporate prefab modules form the original core — gray, modular, functional. Around them, settlers have begun building with salvaged materials and local wood. It looks like a campsite that decided to become a town. The perimeter wall is the one thing everyone agrees needs to stay standing.

---

## Geography

Firstfall sits on a coastal bluff overlooking **Perihelion Bay** — a natural deepwater harbor formed from the flooded southern rim of an ancient impact crater. The crater is approximately 50,000 years old. Corporate geological surveys identified it from orbit: high mineral concentration in the surrounding terrain from impact ejecta, plus a sheltered port in the same location. Corporate did not choose this site by accident.

The mineral-rich ejecta from the impact is scattered across the terrain to the north and west, concentrated most heavily within 5–10 miles of Firstfall. Surface deposits — accessible without deep mining — are the colony's early industrial base. The serious deposits lie at the **impact center**, several miles north, where the geology is dense and strange and the Coherence is wrong.

The impact zone's center is Coherence-dead. Whatever ecosystem existed before the strike was obliterated. What's grown back in 50,000 years is younger, thinner, and the Coherence signal there is weaker and stranger than anywhere else on the planet. Corporate's initial models suggested this would make the zone less hostile to colonists. At the one-year mark, that assumption is being quietly revisited.

Quantum transports achieve orbit and ferry colonists down via shuttle. The **shuttle pad** sits just outside the South Gate, adjacent to the harbor docks. The harbor itself currently hosts only a handful of small watercraft — fishing boats, a coastal scout — but the infrastructure for more is planned.

## The Perimeter

Firstfall is walled. Not elegantly — the wall is Corporate security fencing reinforced by whatever settlers could bolt to it in the first year. Raw, functional, and in two places visibly temporary. It has four gates, one per cardinal direction.

| Gate | Direction | Terrain Beyond | Status |
|------|-----------|----------------|--------|
| **South Gate** | South | Shuttle pad, Perihelion Bay harbor | Always staffed. Primary Corporate entry. Shuttles, supply runs, new arrivals. |
| **North Gate** | North | Impact debris field, surface mineral deposits | Staffed. Most-used working gate. Resource runs go out here daily. Risky. |
| **East Gate** | East | Coastal scrubland, lower Coherence pressure | Staffed during daylight, locked at night. Exploration, fishing access. |
| **West Gate** | West | Continental interior, unbroken Coherence | Restricted. Requires Corporate clearance or established reputation. Nobody goes far. |

The developed area extends roughly a mile out from the wall to the north and east — where resource runs have beaten paths into the terrain and the Coherence hasn't fully reclaimed the ground. Beyond that mile, you are on your own.

## Facility Areas

### 1. The Shuttle Pad, Harbor & Cryo-Bay
*(New player entry point)*

Outside the South Gate, at the edge of Perihelion Bay. The shuttle pad is a reinforced landing area for the atmospheric ferries that carry colonists down from the transports in orbit. Adjacent to the pad, the cryo-bay is a prefab structure where arriving colonists revive after transit. Below the bluff, the harbor docks extend into the bay — currently home to a handful of fishing boats and a coastal scout vessel. The water is the wrong color. The things moving in it are not fish.

**Key rooms:**
- Cryo-Bay (pod rows, revival stations)
- Cargo Bay 3 (player lockers, personal effects)
- Shuttle Corridor (path toward the ramp)
- Disembarkation Ramp (first view of the planet — **critical room**: the bay, the bluff, The Eye on the horizon)
- Harbor Dock (brief exposure to the bay before the path up to the South Gate)
- Approach Path (the walk up the bluff from the harbor to the South Gate)

**Key NPCs:**
- *Revival Technician* — Corporate employee, bored, processes arrivals by rote
- *The Voice* — the recorded announcement system. Not a person.
- *Dock Worker Hess* — maintains the boats, knows the bay, will tell you what's in the water if you ask the right way

---

### 2. The Receiving
*(Newbie orientation zone)*

Just inside the Main Gate. A Corporate processing facility that functions as the tutorial zone. New arrivals are funneled here, assessed, assigned a bunk, briefed on regulations, and released into Firstfall proper. The staff are overworked. The briefings are perfunctory. You learn more from the other new arrivals waiting in line than from anything official.

**Key rooms:**
- Intake Lobby (queue, waiting, NPCs from various professions)
- Assessment Office (profession verified, Corporate ID issued)
- Briefing Room (basic orientation — in-game tutorial wrapper)
- Medical Screening (basic health check, opportunity for Medical profession to shine)
- Equipment Issue (standard-issue survival pack issued — minimal)
- Assignment Board (bunk assignment, initial job postings)
- Holding Area (the unofficial room where people who've been processed but aren't sure what to do next end up)

**Key NPCs:**
- *Intake Officer Reyes* — Corporate, efficient, slightly sympathetic
- *Sergeant Okafor* — security intake, Military profession contact
- *Dr. Vasquez* — medical intake, Medical profession contact
- *Old Maren* — a settler who somehow got permission to sit in the waiting area and talk to new arrivals. Unofficial orientation. Tells you things Corporate doesn't.

---

### 3. Corporate Administration
*(Faction hub — Corporate)*

The original prefab command structure. Sterile, organized, color-coded. The political center of Corporate's operation on the planet. Also the most hated building in Firstfall.

**Key rooms:**
- Administration Lobby
- Commerce Office (trader licensing, contracts, Corporate employment board)
- Communications Center (quantum comms access — expensive, monitored)
- Security Office (law enforcement, complaints, permits)
- The Director's Suite (upper level — restricted access)
- Records Room (colony history, Corporate files — some accessible, some not)

**Key NPCs:**
- *Director Chen* — Corporate authority figure. Politically careful. Not evil, just Corporate.
- *Comms Officer Valdez* — runs the quantum relay. Knows everything that comes through.
- *Permit Clerk* — processes licenses, trade permits, access requests. Obstinate by policy.

---

### 4. The Medical Facility
*(Essential services)*

The best-equipped building in Firstfall, because Corporate needs its workers alive. Settler-accessible but the wait is long and the bill is real. A back room exists for those who know to ask for it — cheaper, less official, staffed by a Synchronized healer whose methods Corporate would rather not examine.

**Key rooms:**
- Reception / Waiting Room
- Treatment Bay (primary care, wound treatment)
- Surgery (serious injuries)
- Pharmacy (supplies, prescriptions — some requiring licenses)
- The Back Room (unofficial, Synchronized healer, ask the right people)
- Medical Storage (locked — accessible to Medical profession, or with skill)

**Key NPCs:**
- *Chief Medic Osei* — competent, overworked, drinks too much
- *Nurse Practitioner Tam* — warm, efficient, knows which settlers to trust
- *The Healer* (no given name used) — Synchronized, back room, treats with methods that work and leaves no records

---

### 5. The Engineering Bay
*(Crafting and repair hub)*

The largest structure in Firstfall after the landing pad. Fabrication equipment, repair stations, salvage intake, and the colony's only functioning forge. Crowded, loud, smells like burnt metal and something biological that nobody talks about.

**Key rooms:**
- Main Workshop (general repairs, fabrication benches)
- The Forge (metalworking — key crafting station)
- Electronics Bench (tech repair, components)
- Salvage Yard (outside, attached — items stripped for parts)
- Parts Storage (locked — Engineer access or skill)
- The Quiet Corner (where the one serious Engineer goes to work on things they won't discuss)

**Key NPCs:**
- *Chief Engineer Dax* — settler, been here since near the start, doesn't trust Corporate equipment
- *Apprentice Crew* (multiple) — rotating cast of junior engineers, quest-givers
- *The Tinkerer* — Underworld-adjacent, makes things Corporate didn't approve

---

### 6. The Supply Depot
*(General store — Corporate)*

Where Corporate issues equipment and sells supplies. Overpriced by frontier standards. The selection is whatever came on the last ship. The quality is reliable. They don't negotiate.

**Key rooms:**
- Sales Floor (equipment, rations, basics)
- Restricted Stock (weapons — license required)
- Back Warehouse (inventory — periodic shortages when ships are late)

**Key NPCs:**
- *Depot Manager Hollis* — Corporate employee, tracks inventory obsessively
- *Supply Runner* — makes runs between the depot and Engineering Bay, knows everyone

---

### 7. The Canteen / Mess Hall
*(Social hub — neutral ground)*

The only place in Firstfall where Corporate staff, settlers, Adapted, and new arrivals all occupy the same room. The food is adequate. The coffee is real and therefore precious. The conversations are where you learn what's actually happening in Firstfall and beyond the wall.

**Key rooms:**
- Main Hall (tables, bar counter, posted notices)
- Kitchen (cooks, quest source for Agricultural profession)
- The Corner Booth (where the real conversations happen — occupied by someone important most of the time)
- Back Porch (outside, attached — faces east, view of the bush, where people go to think)

**Key NPCs:**
- *Cook Fatima* — runs the kitchen, has opinions about everything, knows everyone
- *Barkeep Soro* — neutral, keeps secrets, source of rumors
- *Various regulars* — settler veterans, Corporate off-duty, new arrivals finding their feet

---

### 8. The Barracks
*(Housing)*

Bunks for new arrivals and transient workers. Not comfortable. Not permanent. Where you sleep until you've been here long enough to arrange something better.

**Key rooms:**
- New Arrival Wing (player starting bunk assignment)
- General Barracks (long-term transient housing)
- Corporate Staff Quarters (separate wing — better, access restricted)
- Common Room (small, shared, where people decompress)

---

### 9. The Settler Quarter
*(Faction hub — Settlers and Adapted)*

The organic, unplanned section of Firstfall. Buildings built over time from salvaged materials, repaired and expanded by whoever needed space. Feels different from the Corporate prefabs — warmer, more layered, more dangerous if you don't know the social rules.

**Key rooms:**
- The Quarter Main Street (central thoroughfare)
- The Unofficial Market (trading, no licenses, no Corporate oversight)
- The Meeting House (settler governance — informal, loud, democratic by necessity)
- The Adapted Corner (quieter, respected, don't stare)
- The Wall Garden (small cultivated space growing local plants — maintained by someone Synchronized, what it's doing to the local Coherence is subtle)

**Key NPCs:**
- *Elder Rourke* — old settler, Long-Stayed, not Bonded but Adapted naturally. Respected by everyone including Corporate, who won't admit it.
- *The Traders* (multiple) — settler merchants, barter-friendly, have things the depot doesn't
- *Mira* — young, arrived on The Perihelion at age nine. Has been here the entire year. The first Groundborn children don't exist yet, but Mira is close — the planet has had her since before she was fully formed. Watches new arrivals with unsettling interest.

---

### 10. The Research Station
*(Faction hub — Seekers)*

A converted prefab that the Seekers have covered in sensor equipment, sample storage, and dense data printouts. Corporate tolerates them because their research occasionally produces useful findings. The Seekers tolerate Corporate because Corporate has the quantum comms relay.

**Key rooms:**
- Main Lab (analysis equipment, sample displays)
- Field Preparation Room (where Seekers gear up before going out)
- Data Archive (extensive, partially accessible to players)
- The Roof (sensor array — good view of the sky and The Eye)

**Key NPCs:**
- *Dr. Okafor-Sinh* — lead Seeker, methodical, excited about everything the planet does
- *Field Researcher Lev* — more practical, leads field expeditions, gives quests
- *The Archive* — a data system with a personality that may or may not be the result of someone bonding with it

---

### 11. The Orthodox Gathering
*(Faction hub — Orthodox)*

Not a building Corporate built. A space the Orthodox carved out in the Settler Quarter, expanded over time. Other settlers keep a respectful distance. The space itself feels different — quieter, the air slightly different, as if the Coherence is paying attention here.

**Key rooms:**
- The Threshold (entrance, where newcomers are assessed before being allowed further)
- The Hall (meeting, prayer, community — simple, deliberate)
- The Inner Room (Voiced only — what happens here is not for outsiders)
- The Garden (exterior — plants growing in patterns that don't match what any botanist would plant)

**Key NPCs:**
- *Speaker Davan* — Orthodox leader, The Voiced. Calm in a way that unnerves people. *Knows things.*
- *Keeper Ysolde* — manages the space, assesses newcomers, protective of the Inner Room
- *The Uncertain* — a character wrestling with whether to commit to the Orthodox path. Quest hub.

---

### 12. The Perimeter Wall & Gates
*(Transition zone to the wider world)*

The wall itself is walkable in sections. The gates are the exits to the world — each one a different kind of threshold. Players can walk the wall to observe the terrain before committing to going out.

**Key rooms:**
- South Gate (interior and exterior — primary entry, shuttle traffic)
- North Gate (interior and exterior — working gate, resource runners, most activity)
- East Gate (interior and exterior — quieter, coastal direction)
- West Gate (interior and exterior — restricted, heavily watched)
- Wall Walk North (view of the debris field and the impact terrain beyond)
- Wall Walk East (view of the coast and the bay)

**Key NPCs:**
- *Gate Guard Priya* — South Gate, processes arrivals, tired but decent, full of practical advice
- *Runner Kade* — spends most of his time at the North Gate, organizes resource runs, hires people
- *Gate Watch Fen* — East Gate, quiet, has been watching the coastal treeline for a year and seen things she doesn't fully discuss
- *Corporal Westing* — West Gate, Corporate, takes the restriction seriously, not cruel about it

## Atmosphere Notes

Firstfall should feel like a place people *live*, not a game lobby. The NPCs have routines. The canteen is busier at mealtimes. The wall guards change shift. The Coherence is faint here — suppressed by human infrastructure — but not absent. On quiet nights, in the right parts of the Settler Quarter, you can almost feel it.

New players should feel oriented but not safe. Protected but not comfortable. The wall is real. What's outside it is more real.

The Eye is always visible. Day or night, when the sky is clear, it's there. Buildings are oriented so most windows face away from it, but you always know which direction it is. Settlers know without looking.
