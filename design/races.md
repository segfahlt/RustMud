# Races

Status: **Placeholder** — awaiting theme/lore decisions

---

## What This Is

Races define the biological/cultural identity of a character at creation. They provide stat modifiers, innate abilities, equipment restrictions, and flavor. Race is permanent — chosen once at character creation and cannot be changed (except via remort, potentially).

---

## What Races Typically Provide

| Feature | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Stat modifiers | +2 Strength, -1 Intelligence |
| Stat caps | Max Strength 22 instead of 20 |
| Innate abilities | Infravision, water breathing, natural armor |
| Size | Small, Medium, Large — affects carry weight, equipment, combat |
| Resistances | Fire resistance, poison immunity |
| Vulnerabilities | Weakness to silver, sunlight penalty |
| Class restrictions | Some races can't be certain classes |
| Starting language | Racial tongue in addition to common |
| Lore flavor | Description shown at character creation |

---

## Key Design Questions

- What races exist? Entirely **theme-dependent** — classic (human, elf, dwarf, halfling) vs. something original.
- Are there "evil" races that are hostile-by-default to players? (pkill races)
- Does race affect the remort system? (Some MUDs let you change race on remort)
- Are there rare/locked races unlocked by achievements or remort?
- Does size affect combat in a meaningful mechanical way, or just flavor?
- How many races is too many? (3–5 well-differentiated > 10 that blur together)
- Do racial innate abilities use the same environment-manipulation system, or are they separate?

---

## Dependencies

- Theme/lore (what races exist in this world and why)
- Player stats (racial modifiers apply here)
- Classes (race/class restrictions)
- Environment manipulation (innate abilities may use same mechanics)
- Character creation flow (race selection screen)
