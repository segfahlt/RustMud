# Character Creation

Status: **Draft** — flow and professions defined, skill system pending class/stats design

---

## Overview

Character creation happens before the player enters the world. It establishes who they were on Earth and what they brought with them. It does not lock their future — the planet changes people. A military contractor can become a Synchronized mystic. A scientist can become a settler farmer. Profession is origin, not destiny.

Creation is three steps:

1. **Name** — who are you?
2. **Profession** — what did you do on Earth?
3. **Personal items** — what did you bring?

After creation, the arrival narrative plays and they wake in the cryo-bay.

---

## Step 1 — Name

Standard name validation: 3–24 letters only. Globally unique across all characters.

No title, no prefix. Just a name. The world will give them a reputation in time.

---

## Step 2 — Profession

What the player did on Earth. Determines:
- Starting skills (3–4 skills at novice-to-competent level)
- Starting gear in their locker (profession kit, Corporate-issued)
- Arrival flavor text variation (a soldier's pod is labeled differently than a scientist's)

Profession does **not** determine class, lock abilities, or restrict future development. It is a starting point with mechanical weight, not a cage.

### Profession List

---

#### Military / Security
*Corporate security contractor, discharged soldier, law enforcement — someone who spent their career pointing weapons at problems.*

**Starting skills:** Weapons (rifles), Combat (armed), Tactics, Physical Conditioning
**Locker contents:** Corporate-issue light armor, service rifle (limited ammo), combat knife, field rations ×5, Corporate employment contract (flimsy)
**Arrival note:** *Your transit agreement includes a security service clause. Report to Sergeant Okafor in The Receiving upon processing.*

---

#### Medical
*Ship medic, civilian doctor, trauma surgeon, field paramedic — someone who kept people alive professionally.*

**Starting skills:** Medicine, Diagnosis, Field Surgery, Pharmacology
**Locker contents:** Medical kit (standard), diagnostic scanner (basic), antiseptic supplies, surgical tools, reference data chip (Earth medical)
**Arrival note:** *The colony medical facility is understaffed. Your skills have been flagged as priority placement.*

---

#### Engineer / Technical
*Mechanical engineer, systems technician, electrician, fabricator — someone who makes things work and keeps them working.*

**Starting skills:** Fabrication, Electronics, Mechanical Repair, Systems Analysis
**Locker contents:** Tool kit (comprehensive), diagnostic multi-tool, schematics data chip, spare components (assorted), work gloves
**Arrival note:** *Engineering intake is in Bay 7. Bring your credentials chip — they'll want to see your certification rating.*

## Step 3 — Personal Items

Players choose **5 items** from the list below. These are Earth goods — carried across eighteen months of transit, stored in cryo alongside their owner. They are irreplaceable. No equivalent exists on the planet. No shop sells them. When they're gone, they're gone.

The choice is mechanical and narrative. A soldier who brought seeds and a photograph plays differently than one who brought two extra weapons and a lockpick set. The mix tells a story.

### Item List

**Weapons**
- `Hunting knife` — quality steel, full tang. Tool and weapon both.
- `Personal sidearm` — civilian model, Earth-manufactured. Comes with one spare magazine. Ammo is limited and cannot be resupplied locally.
- `Compound bow` — collapsible, silent. Arrows can be crafted from local materials once you know what you're doing.
- `Combat baton` — expandable, non-lethal. Security professionals favor these.

**Tools**
- `Multi-tool` — worn, reliable, seventeen functions. The good kind.
- `Climbing gear` — harness, cams, 30m of rope. Earth-spec, rated for serious terrain.
- `Survival kit` — fire starter, emergency blanket, water purification tablets, signal mirror.
- `Lock override kit` — commercial grade. Gets you through most Corporate-standard doors.

**Medical**
- `Personal medical kit` — beyond the basics. Wound closure, IV supplies, stronger analgesics than Corporate issues to everyone.
- `Stimulant pack` — emergency energy. Three doses. Not a long-term solution.
- `Prescription medication` — for a condition the player had on Earth. *(Pure roleplay. No game effect. Describe it yourself.)*

**Technology**
- `Knowledge data chip` — a specialty database from Earth. Choose the field when you select this item: engineering, biology, history, law, chemistry, etc. Skill reference bonus.
- `Encrypted personal comms unit` — allows private communication outside Corporate channels. Technically requires a license.
- `Quality hand scanner` — older model but better than the colony-issue equipment. Wider range, more accurate.
- `Personal recorder` — captures audio and video. Useful for documentation, evidence, or memory.

**Provisions**
- `Earth alcohol` — a bottle of something real. Trades extremely well. One use as a social lubricant, or keep it.
- `Earth food rations ×10` — familiar taste. Finite. More valuable than they sound after six weeks on colony rations.
- `Earth seeds (personal)` — something from home. Unlikely to survive local soil, but you brought them anyway. *(Primarily roleplay; potential crafting ingredient.)*

**Personal / Sentimental**
- `Family photograph` — physical print. No game effect. You write the description.
- `Musical instrument` — small enough to pack. Harmonica, travel guitar, hand drum. Social uses; can busk in the canteen.
- `Religious text or artifact` — physical, personal. Relevant to your faction alignment or purely personal. No game effect.
- `A letter` — written to you, or by you, unsent. No game effect. You write the description.
- `Lucky charm` — minor, persistent luck modifier. Or maybe it's just a rock. You've kept it this long.
- `Earth soil (sealed)` — a small container of dirt from a specific place. Entirely sentimental. Some people need to know they can still touch it.
- `Paper book` — actual paper. Rare enough to be remarkable. Choose the title; it may provide knowledge or social value.

## The Locker Scene

After the arrival narrative, the player is directed to their locker in Cargo Bay 3. The locker contains:

- Their **profession kit** (Corporate-issued, waiting for them)
- Their **5 personal items** (in the condition they packed them)
- A **transit manifest** (their name, profession, Corporate ID, and a brief note from the colony)
- A **standard-issue survival guide** (basic orientation pamphlet — links to in-game help system)

The locker description changes based on profession. A soldier's locker has a different feel than a scientist's. The personal items are listed with brief descriptions when the player looks at them — the photograph, the letter, the charm — giving the player a moment of quiet characterization before the world begins in earnest.

---

## The Walk to The Receiving

From the cargo bay, the player moves through the ship toward the disembarkation ramp. This is a short linear sequence — three or four rooms — designed to show them the ship, other passengers (background NPCs), and build tension before the planet.

Key moment: **the top of the ramp**. The player exits the ship's controlled environment and sees Firstfall and the sky for the first time. The Eye is visible. The air smells wrong. The room description here should be one of the most carefully written in the game.

From the ramp, a path leads to The Receiving. The walk is short but exposed — a first taste of the planet's light and atmosphere before the shelter of the base.
