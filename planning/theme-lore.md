# Theme and Lore — The Eye

Status: **Active** — world bible in progress. Update as decisions are made.

---

## The MUD

**Name:** The Eye
**Inspiration:** W. Michael Gear's Donovan series — used as a creative springboard, not a copy. The world, its mechanics, its factions, and its metaphysics are original.
**Tone:** Dark frontier sci-fi. Survival, discovery, and slow transformation. Not horror, not heroic fantasy — something in between. The world is not evil. It is indifferent to you until you force it not to be.

---

## The World

### The Eye (the black hole)
A stellar-mass black hole, the gravitational anchor of the system. The planet's star orbits it. The Eye is always visible in the sky — a darkness ringed with the blue-white fire of its accretion disk, occasionally flaring. Settlers grow up under it. It is the dominant cultural reference point for everyone on the planet.

Corporate designation: **SGR-1847** (never used by anyone who actually lives here)
Settler name: **The Eye**

The Eye warps the local quantum field in ways that make faster-than-light inversion calculations unreliable. Ships go missing here at a rate Corporate quietly does not publish. This is why the world was not colonized sooner despite being habitable. The math keeps killing the ships.

### The Planet
The planet has a Corporate catalog designation nobody uses. Settlers refer to it as "the planet," "here," or gesture at The Eye when asked where they are. The Eye has consumed the role a planet name would normally fill. Giving the planet its own name marks you as someone who just arrived.

**Characteristics:**
- Habitable but actively hostile to unmodified human biology
- Ecosystem extraordinarily complex and interconnected
- Flora and fauna alien enough that Earth biology provides almost no useful intuition
- The deeper you go from Firstfall, the more the world has never seen a human
- Resources exist — some extraordinary, some dangerous to extract — but the planet does not yield them passively

### The Impact Site and Perihelion Bay

Approximately 50,000 years ago, a large asteroid struck the planet's northern coast. The impact created a massive crater. Over millennia the southern rim was flooded by the sea, forming **Perihelion Bay** — a natural deepwater harbor sheltered from open ocean. Corporate geological surveys identified the site from orbit before The Perihelion ever launched: the impact ejecta left mineral-rich surface deposits across the surrounding terrain for miles in every direction, and the bay provided a viable port. Corporate chose this exact site deliberately.

**Ejecta field (surface):** Mineral deposits scattered north and west of Firstfall, concentrated within 5–10 miles. Accessible without deep mining equipment. The colony's early industrial base. Essential for establishing any local industry in year one.

**Impact center (deep):** Several miles north of Firstfall. The serious deposits — dense, concentrated, geologically strange — lie here. Reaching them requires infrastructure, planning, and dealing with the fact that the Coherence at the impact center is wrong.

**Coherence-dead zone:** The impact obliterated whatever ecosystem existed at the center. What grew back in 50,000 years is younger, thinner, and less integrated with the broader Coherence. The signal there is weaker and stranger than anywhere else. Corporate's initial models suggested lower ecosystem resistance in this zone. One year in, that assumption is being quietly revisited — something is using the weakness differently than expected.

## The Metaphysics

### The Coherence
The local term for the quantum biotic network underlying all life on the planet. Every organism is quantum-entangled with others in its biome. Not a brain, not a central will — a standing wave of correlated biological states spanning the entire ecosystem. Disturb one part and the system instantly correlates across the network.

Corporate scientific designation: **Quantum Biotic Network (QBN)** — a phenomenon, not an intelligence.
Settler term: **The Coherence** — used matter-of-factly, the way you'd refer to weather.

The Coherence is why the ecosystem responds to human presence as a unified system rather than individual organisms. It is also why symbiont bonding means something deeper than biology: bonding begins to entangle human nervous systems with the local network. Over time, bonded individuals change.

The black hole amplifies and distorts quantum effects in the local region of space. This makes The Coherence stronger here than it would be on any other habitable world — and makes the planet more alive, more responsive, and more dangerous than Corporate models predicted.

### The Shaper
Something shaped the life on this world. The Coherence did not evolve entirely on its own — it has been cultivated, directed, refined over geological timescales by something that experiences time and causality differently than matter does.

The Shaper is singular. Not a species, not a committee, not a force — something that does not map cleanly to any of those categories. It exists in dimensional layers that the black hole makes accessible. The event horizon is thin space. The Coherence is its medium — possibly its voice, possibly its nervous system — depending on who you ask and how much they've bonded.

**What The Shaper is** is the central unanswered question of the world. No faction has definitive proof. The Shaper does not communicate in language. It shaped. It may still be shaping. Whether it is aware of individual humans — whether it has noticed the colony at all — is unknown and deeply argued.

### Humans as Irritants
Humans are not enemies of the planet. They are the wrong frequency. Every engine, every explosion, every radio transmission, every unmodified human heartbeat is slightly out of phase with the Coherence's resonant state. The ecosystem responds the way any coherent system responds to noise: it tries to dampen it.

Mob aggression is not malice. It is inflammation. The planet is not trying to kill colonists — it is trying to reduce interference. The distinction matters because it means the conflict is solvable. Not through warfare. Through *change.*

Symbiont bonding changes a human's quantum signature over time. The more bonded a person becomes, the less friction they generate. Deeply bonded characters eventually become low-priority to the ecosystem's immune response. The most synchronized individuals become effectively invisible to it — not because they've beaten the planet, but because the planet has stopped classifying them as foreign.

## The People

### Origins — The Landing

The first colony ship to successfully dock was **The Perihelion** — named by its crew for the point in an orbit closest to the gravitational center. The joke being that flying near The Eye's quantum gradient made every other danger in the universe feel manageable. Among settlers, "pulling a perihelion" means getting lucky when you had no right to.

New players arrive aboard the latest Corporate transport — in orbit, ferried down by shuttle to the pad at Perihelion Bay. The base is **Port Meridian** to Corporate, **Firstfall** to everyone else. They carry what they brought. Nothing more. The planet is out those gates.

**Colony age:** 1 year. The wall is up. The prefabs are standing. ~700 people are alive who weren't born here. The first Groundborn children do not yet exist. The Adapted caste is barely forming — a handful of people from The Perihelion's original crew who've started to notice things about themselves they don't fully have words for.

### World Entry — Arrival Narrative

*(This text is shown to new players as their character wakes from cryo-sleep)*

The world comes back in pieces.

Cold first — bone-deep, the kind that lived inside you for eighteen months of transit. Then pale blue-white light through your eyelids. Then sound: cryo-gas venting, the distant thrum of ship systems, someone coughing three pods down.

Your pod opens.

The ceiling above you is brushed aluminum, scratched by the hands of every previous occupant. Stenciled in fading yellow: POD 7-DELTA. Below that, someone has scratched a single word with something sharp.

*SORRY.*

A recorded voice — calm, Corporate-neutral — drops from the speaker overhead:

*"Welcome to Kepler-7711. Local designation: Port Meridian. Please proceed to your assigned locker in Cargo Bay 3 to collect your personal effects. Intake processing begins in forty minutes. Failure to report within two hours of revival constitutes a breach of your transit agreement."*

You sit up slowly. Through the porthole beside your pod, past scratched plexiglass and the dark silhouette of the hull, the sky is the wrong color. Not the blue you grew up with. Something deeper — violet, almost — lit from an angle that doesn't match where the sun should be. And there, low on the horizon, a darkness ringed with white fire.

You saw it in the briefings. Pictures don't do it justice.

*The Eye.*

It watches.

## The Factions

### Corporate
The original authority. Controls Firstfall's infrastructure, the quantum comms relay, and the official record of who owns what. Corporate does not acknowledge The Shaper. The Coherence is a documented phenomenon with no intelligence behind it. The Adapted are biologically compromised colonists requiring medical monitoring. The Synchronized are a cult.

Corporate's interest is extraction and expansion. The planet's resistance is a logistics problem to be engineered around. *Relationship to systems: controls high tech, medical gear, quantum comms, new player processing.*

### The Settlers
The practical middle. Been here long enough to know Corporate's models are wrong, not long enough or not willing to go the way of the Adapted. They know the bush. They have rules about it. They trade with Corporate when useful, ignore them when not, and have buried enough people to have opinions about what works.

Settlers are the largest group and the least unified. The Coherence is real to them in a practical sense — you feel it when you've been out long enough. *Relationship to systems: mixed tech and local knowledge. Core player population.*

### The Adapted
Those who changed without fully choosing it. Born on the planet, or lived here long enough that the body accommodated the Coherence passively. Faster reflexes. Stronger instincts. Some sense shifts in local ecosystem behavior before they're visible. The Adapted did not seek bonding — the planet found them acceptable enough to begin incorporating. *Relationship to systems: reduced ecosystem strife, early symbiont abilities, social status among non-Corporate.*

### The Synchronized
Those who sought bonding deliberately. Faster and deeper than passive adaptation, and more dangerous — pushing the interface too hard, too fast, has broken people. Those who came through intact are something else. Quieter. More deliberate. They perceive things others don't. They do not argue about The Shaper. *Relationship to systems: deep symbiont abilities, high ecosystem trust, low tech dependency, informal caste structure.*

### The Orthodox
A religious faction crystallized around The Shaper. They believe it is conscious, intentional, and that The Eye is its instrument of observation. The Coherence is its voice. The Adapted and Synchronized are what humans are meant to become. Corporate is a desecration. Not violent by doctrine, but adversarial with Corporate and unnervingly collaborative with the ecosystem. *Relationship to systems: deep Coherence access, caste structure based on proximity to The Shaper's perceived intent.*

### The Seekers
Secular scientists and obsessives who believe The Shaper is real but knowable only through method, not faith. The Coherence is the signal. Deeper bonding brings clearer reception. Their goal is to understand and, eventually, communicate. At odds with both Corporate (who denies The Shaper) and the Orthodox (who think understanding it is presumptuous). *Relationship to systems: moderate bonding, research-oriented, occasionally useful to everyone and trusted by none.*

## The Caste Structure

Among the Adapted, Synchronized, and Orthodox, a natural hierarchy emerged that no one designed:

| Caste | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Groundborn** | Born on the planet. The Coherence knows them from birth. |
| **The Long-Stayed** | Arrived early, adapted over decades. Earned their standing. |
| **The Bonded** | Deliberately Synchronized. Depth of bonding determines rank. |
| **The Voiced** | Those who claim clear signal from The Shaper. Rare. Disputed. Powerful. |

Corporate recognizes none of this. Settlers respect it without fully understanding it. The Orthodox treat The Voiced as clergy.

## The World Events System

The planet responds to player actions through the **Coherence threat level** — a global state variable that rises as colonists damage the ecosystem and falls as the Adapted/Synchronized work to reduce friction.

| Threat Level | Ecosystem Response |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Low | Normal mob behavior, routine encounters |
| Elevated | Increased aggression on wilderness routes, unusual mob coordination |
| High | Organized campaigns: mobs moving with evident coordination toward settlements |
| Critical | Full ecosystem mobilization — world event, server-wide coordination needed |

**Quantum ship arrivals** are also world events: supply surges, Corporate pressure increases, new colonists arrive, news from wider human civilization reaches the planet. Ship losses create the opposite: supply shortages, tech prices spike, Corporate becomes authoritarian and paranoid.

## The Power Systems

### Technology (Corporate)
High-powered, requires infrastructure, breaks down, depends on supply ships. Weapons, medical gear, communications, vehicles, fabrication equipment. Scarce outside Firstfall. Corporate controls distribution. A tech-dependent character is powerful but fragile.

### Symbiont Bonding (The Coherence)
Organic abilities that develop through relationship with the planet. Not learned — grown. Depth of bonding determines capability: early bonding grants instincts and ecosystem awareness; deep bonding grants active abilities (accelerated healing, sensory extension, ecosystem communication); the deepest levels access something that Corporate would classify as impossible and the Orthodox would call communion with The Shaper.

Bonding cannot be rushed without risk. Pushing too hard produces cascade failure — the human nervous system cannot handle the entanglement load.

### The Shaper's Signal (Endgame)
At the deepest levels of bonding, something else becomes perceptible through the Coherence. Not The Coherence itself — something using it. Whether this is The Shaper communicating, the Coherence developing emergent self-awareness, or something the mind is constructing from quantum noise is left deliberately ambiguous. Abilities available at this level are extraordinary and not fully understood even by those who have them.
