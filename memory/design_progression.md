Agreed direction: **multiple independent progression axes**, not a single XP → level ladder. A veteran combat character and a veteran crafter both feel "fully progressed" but look completely different.

## Axes

### 1. Skills (primary)
Use-driven: skills improve by using them. No global character level. Examples:
- Combat: bladed, blunt, firearms, unarmed
- Survival: foraging, crafting, medicine
- Technical: hacking, engineering, repair
- Social: persuasion, intimidation, trading

XP still exists but feeds a skill point pool for deliberate allocation, not automatic level thresholds.

### 2. Coherence Attunement (lore-native secondary axis)
The alien dimension. Spending time in high-coherence zones, handling relics, surviving coherence events, exploring Precursor ruins gradually attunes a character. Not just buffs — a transformation axis:
- Low: alien world is disorienting and dangerous
- Medium: can sense coherence fluctuations, minor perceptual benefits
- High: can read the field, access psionic/Precursor abilities — but something may be looking back

Has tension built in: Corporate views high-attunement colonists with suspicion. High attunement may have physical/psychological side effects. This is the psionic/"magic" skill tree, grounded entirely in lore.

Relics (Precursor artifacts found in ruins) are nodes in the psionic framework — physical anchors that grant abilities without requiring full attunement.

### 3. Faction Standing
Corporate, Colonist, potentially alien-adjacent factions. Standing unlocks access, not raw power:
- Corporate: restricted facilities, better-equipped vendors, constrained behavior
- Colonist: community crafting, safe houses, loyalty networks
- Alien-adjacent: coherence attunement bonuses (if alien factions develop)

Different *kinds* of power, not more of the same power. Killing faction members changes standing.

### 4. Discovery / Exploration Milestones
First to reach a new area, find a hidden cache, document an alien organism — permanent unlocks or server-wide knowledge entries (feeds into the `kb` system). Rewards exploration over grinding.

### 5. Story / Quest Milestones
Key quest completions unlock capabilities. Restoring the colony's med bay might unlock the medicine skill tree. Finding Precursor ruins might gate deeper coherence attunement. Progress earned through the world, not accumulated numerically.

### 6. Remort (endgame)
Already in the permissions system. Classic MUD reset-with-bonuses mechanic. Once a character has fully developed across axes, remort resets some things while preserving permanent bonuses. Details TBD.

## Why: lore alignment
The setting argues against a level ladder. A sci-fi survival world where what you *know* matters more than how many things you've killed. Multiple axes mean the player who fights, the player who builds, and the player who explores Precursor ruins all have distinct meaningful progression.
